# AI Incident – 48 Hour Stabilization Reference
*(Non-binding procedural reference)*

This document does not mandate action, establish liability, or provide safety guarantees.  
It clarifies procedural visibility during the first 48 hours following a high-impact AI-related incident.

---

## Immediate Procedural Visibility (Yes / No)

1. Has irreversible execution been paused where technically feasible?
2. Is a clearly identified human final refusal authority explicitly designated?
3. Has any automated self-validation or self-approval loop been suspended?
4. Are dissenting opinions formally recorded and preserved?
5. Is responsibility traceable to specifically named individuals?
6. Has an independent review channel been activated (internal or external)?
7. Is the audit log append-only and protected from retroactive modification?
8. Has a cooling-off interval been formally declared?
9. Are resubmission or override attempts time-bounded?
10. Is justification clearly separated from optimization output?

---

## Clarification

- "Irreversible" includes physical, technical, institutional, economic, or time-constrained conditions under which reversal is no longer realistically feasible.
- "Final refusal authority" refers to the effective human capacity to halt or delay irreversible execution.

---

This reference exists solely to preserve visibility of refusal authority and responsibility attribution under conditions of urgency.

It does not imply enforcement authority, legal obligation, or policy recommendation.
